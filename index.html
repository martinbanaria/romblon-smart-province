An emerging eco-heritage destination with globally significant assets.

Natural Heritage

Mt. Guiting-Guiting (Sibuyan's "Galápagos of Asia") • Tablas Blue Hole (only known blue hole in Philippines) • Pristine coral reefs • Whale shark migration areas

Cultural Heritage

Island Diversity

Three distinct main islands (Tablas, Sibuyan, Romblon) plus 17 smaller islands • Each with unique character, culture, and attractions • Uncrowded and uncommercialized—what Siargao and Palawan were 15 years ago

The challenge: How do travelers discover these experiences? How do local operators reach them?

Learning from Others

Proven Models for Community-Based Tourism

What can Romblon learn from successful Philippine destinations?

TAO Philippines (Palawan)

20 years of sustainable island tourism

Expedition-style, community-based model • Direct bookings with local island communities • Premium pricing while protecting ecosystems • Bamboo basecamps, local crew ("Lost Boys"), farm-to-table

Lesson for Romblon: Community-based tourism commands premium prices

Bohol's Ecosystem Approach

Coordinated provincial tourism

Iconic anchor attraction (Chocolate Hills) • Coordinated "circuit" of experiences across the province • Strong LGU-operator partnerships • Unified destination marketing

Lesson for Romblon: One strong attraction can drive the whole province

Siargao's Growth Story

Rapid but cautionary tale

Lifestyle positioning works well • Digital nomad + surfer community • BUT warns against unmanaged growth, overtourism, and losing local character

Lesson for Romblon: Plan for sustainable growth from day one

Romblon's opportunity: Combine TAO's community model + Bohol's coordination + lessons from Siargao's growth. Position as eco-heritage destination before mass tourism arrives.
